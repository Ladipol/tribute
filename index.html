Tribute to Great Obafemi Awolowo

The best president Nigeria never had.

From left to right, they are those of Chief Obafemi Awolowo, Dr Nnamdi Azikiwe, Alhaji Tafawa Balewa, Dr E.M.L. Endeley and Alhaji Ahmadu Bello, the Sardauna of Sokoto

Here's a time line of Chief Awolowo's life:

  • 1909 - Was born in Ikene, Ogun State, Nigeria.

  • 1927 - Attended Wesley College, Ibadan,Oyo State, Nigeria.

  • 1944 - He went to the UK to study law at the University of London.

  • 1946 - Was called to the Bar by the Honorable Society of the Inner Temple.

  • 1949 - He founded the Nigerian Tribune, a private Nigerian newspaper, which he used to spread nationalist consciousness among Nigerians.

  • 1954 - He advocated federalism as the only basis for equitable national integration and, as head of the Action Group, he led demands for a federal constitution, which was introduced as Lyttleton Constitution, following primarily the model proposed by the Western Region delegation led by him.

  • 1959 - Was the first Leader of Government Business and Minister of Local Government and Finance, and first Premier of the Western Region under Nigeria's parliamentary system.

  • 1963 - Was the official Leader of the Opposition in the federal parliament to the Balewa government .

  • 1973 - Presidential candidate of Unity Party of Nigeria in the Presidential election.

  • 1987 - Obafemi Awolowo died in Ikenne, Ogun State, Nigeria at the age of 78.
